About Luis

A Voice for
Our Port & Community

The Port of Brownsville is one of the most important economic engines in South Texas — and every Brownsville family feels its impact.

When the port runs efficiently, operating costs go down, businesses expand, and new companies move in. That means more good-paying jobs for our community and less pressure on local taxpayers. A well-run port doesn't just help industry — it helps every family in Brownsville.

Luis Villarreal is running for Port Commissioner, Place 4, to bring a business-minded approach to port leadership — one focused on cutting red tape, driving down costs, and turning savings into jobs and tax relief for our community.

The Plan

Our Priorities for the Port

Less bureaucracy. Lower costs. More jobs. Stronger infrastructure. A port that works for every Brownsville family.

📋

Streamline Industrial Permitting

Industrial tenants should not face unnecessary delays or multiple layers of bureaucracy before they can build. I will pursue the creation of a Port Building & Inspection Department so companies can handle plan review and inspections directly within the Port.

  • One coordinated process
  • Faster approvals
  • On-site inspections
  • Clear timelines
  • Full compliance with safety and environmental standards
Faster permitting means faster construction — and faster job creation for Brownsville families.
💰

Lower Costs Through Efficiency

The Port owns and manages its land as a unified industrial complex. We should leverage that scale to reduce costs for tenants — and when the Port saves money, that means less burden on local taxpayers.

  • Coordinated bulk utility purchasing
  • Smarter infrastructure planning
  • Exploring Port-wide insurance frameworks
  • Reducing duplication and administrative overhead
Lower costs mean more expansion, more local jobs, and less pressure on Brownsville taxpayers.
🏆

Compete & Win on the Gulf Coast

Ports across Texas and the Gulf Coast are competing for the same investments. Every company we attract means more jobs for our neighbors — in shipbuilding, logistics, steel fabrication, and industrial growth.

  • Predictable timelines
  • Cost-efficient operations
  • Strong but streamlined oversight
  • Business-ready infrastructure
The Port should not move at the speed of paperwork. It should move at the speed of opportunity.
🏠

More Jobs, Lower Taxes

Every dollar the Port saves through smarter operations is a dollar that benefits Brownsville. An efficient port attracts more businesses, creates more jobs, and generates more revenue — reducing the tax burden on local families.

  • Port efficiencies that translate to community savings
  • More industry means a broader tax base
  • Good-paying jobs in shipbuilding, logistics & manufacturing
  • Reinvesting savings into infrastructure, not overhead
When the Port works smarter, Brownsville families pay less and earn more.
🔒

Transparency & Accountability

The Port of Brownsville belongs to the public — and the public deserves to know how it's being run. I will push for greater openness in how decisions are made, how money is spent, and how contracts are awarded.

  • Open and accessible board meetings
  • Public access to port contracts and financials
  • Regular community reporting on port performance
  • Clear accountability for how taxpayer resources are used
Sunlight is the best disinfectant. Our port should have nothing to hide.
🤝

Local Hiring & Workforce Development

When the Port grows, Brownsville families should be first in line for those jobs. I will work to connect our local workforce — through TSC, UTRGV, and trade programs — directly to port employment opportunities.

  • Partner with local colleges and trade schools
  • Prioritize hiring from the Brownsville community
  • Create apprenticeship and training pipelines
  • Support certifications in welding, logistics, heavy equipment & more
Our port should put our people to work first.
🚧

Infrastructure Investment

Aging docks, outdated roads, and limited rail connections hold the Port back from reaching its full potential. Modern infrastructure attracts modern industry — and the better-paying jobs that come with it.

  • Upgrade aging docks and cargo facilities
  • Improve port roads and rail connections
  • Modernize utilities to meet industrial demand
  • Plan infrastructure that supports long-term growth
Better infrastructure today means better jobs tomorrow.
🌎

Grow International Trade

Brownsville sits on one of the most strategic trade corridors in North America. Our port should be leveraging that position to expand cross-border commerce and attract international shippers looking for a reliable Gulf Coast gateway.

  • Strengthen U.S.–Mexico trade partnerships
  • Attract international shipping lines and logistics companies
  • Position Brownsville as a North American trade hub
  • Support nearshoring opportunities for manufacturers
Our location is our advantage. Let's use it.
